# 📚 Documentation Examples

> "Strong documentation is not defined by the length of a comment, but by the clarity of its reasoning."

The following examples demonstrate how the Lumina Documentation Reasoning Framework™ transforms observations into objective, evidence-based documentation.

These examples are fictional and intended solely to demonstrate documentation principles and AI-assisted workflow concepts.

---

# Example 01 — Greeting Standard

## 👀 Observation

The representative greeted the customer approximately 35 seconds into the interaction.

---

## 🎯 Quality Expectation

Customers should receive a timely greeting at the beginning of the interaction.

---

## 🧩 Reasoning

A timely greeting establishes rapport, creates a positive first impression, and communicates attentiveness.

---

## ✍️ AI Draft

The representative greeted the customer approximately 35 seconds into the interaction, exceeding the expected greeting timeframe. Providing a timely greeting helps establish rapport and contributes to a positive customer experience. The representative should greet customers within the expected timeframe while continuing any necessary system preparation.

---

## 🌱 Coaching Opportunity

Practice balancing system preparation with timely customer engagement.

---
# Example 02 — Identity Verification

## 👀 Observation

The representative discussed account information before completing identity verification.

---

## 🎯 Quality Expectation

Identity verification should occur before discussing protected account information.

---

## 🧩 Reasoning

Following verification procedures helps protect customer privacy and supports organizational compliance standards.

---

## ✍️ AI Draft

The representative discussed account information before completing the required identity verification process. Completing verification prior to discussing protected information helps maintain customer privacy and supports compliance with organizational procedures. The representative should complete identity verification before accessing or discussing account details.

---

## 🌱 Coaching Opportunity

Reinforce the importance of completing verification before discussing account-specific information.

# Example 03 — Customer Education

## 👀 Observation

The representative provided a partial explanation of available services.

---

## 🎯 Quality Expectation

Customers should receive complete and accurate information that enables informed decision-making.

---

## 🧩 Reasoning

Incomplete education may lead to confusion or prevent customers from understanding available options.

---

## ✍️ AI Draft

The representative provided an explanation of available services; however, the information was incomplete. Providing complete and accurate education helps customers understand available options and supports informed decision-making. The representative should provide comprehensive information using approved organizational resources when appropriate.

---

## 🌱 Coaching Opportunity

Encourage the representative to verify that all relevant information has been communicated before concluding the explanation.

# Example 04 — Active Listening

## 👀 Observation

The representative allowed the customer to finish speaking, acknowledged their concerns, and responded appropriately.

---

## 🎯 Quality Expectation

Representatives should demonstrate active listening throughout customer interactions.

---

## 🧩 Reasoning

Active listening promotes trust, improves communication, and helps ensure customer needs are accurately understood.

---

## ✍️ AI Draft

The representative demonstrated active listening by allowing the customer to complete their statements, acknowledging their concerns, and responding appropriately throughout the interaction. These behaviors supported effective communication and contributed to a positive customer experience.

---

## 🌱 Coaching Opportunity

Continue reinforcing active listening behaviors while maintaining clear communication.

# 🧠 Patterns Behind Great Documentation

Across every example, the same pattern appears.

Great documentation consistently answers five questions.

1. 👀 What happened?

2. 🎯 Which quality expectation applied?

3. 🧩 Why did it matter?

4. ✍️ What should be documented?

5. 🌱 What coaching opportunity exists?

These five questions form the foundation of the Lumina Documentation Reasoning Framework™.

Rather than asking AI to immediately generate documentation, Lumina encourages evaluators to reason through these questions first, ensuring documentation remains objective, consistent, and focused on continuous improvement.
